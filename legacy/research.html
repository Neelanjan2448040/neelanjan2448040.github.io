Abstract

This study focuses on forecasting low-liquidity financial assets using multiple deep learning factors. The challenge lies in accurately modeling the complex and interconnected relationships between these variables in sparse and volatile financial markets. Traditional models often fail to capture these dynamics effectively.

To address this, a hybrid machine learning approach is proposed, combining statistical and generative models. LSTM is used for capturing temporal dependencies, while TimeGAN handles synthetic sequence generation to augment training data. Ensemble techniques are used to improve prediction performance in sparse data scenarios.

The results demonstrate that TimeGAN-enhanced models significantly outperform standalone individual models, achieving lower prediction errors (RMSE reduced from 0.0009 to 0.0002) and near-perfect accuracy (R² improved to 0.9921). This approach provides a more reliable solution for real-world financial forecasting and decision-making.

Key Findings

TimeGAN models outperform traditional and standalone machine learning models

TimeGAN + LSTM achieves highest accuracy and lowest RMSE (0.0002)

LSTM baseline is limited in capturing complex low-liquidity relationships

Synthetic data augmentation improves prediction reliability in volatile markets

Hybrid architecture effectively captures both long-range trends and short-term variance
